Cookie Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how The Agilists Pvt Ltd ("The Agilists", "we", "us" or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on agilists.co.uk. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, to improve efficiency, and to provide information to site owners. Similar technologies such as pixels and local storage work in comparable ways. We refer to all of these as "cookies" in this policy.

2. The cookies we use

Under the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR), we may set strictly necessary cookies without consent, but we will only set analytics or other non-essential cookies after you have given consent.

At the time of writing, the Site does not set any cookies by default. The analytics cookies described below are set only if and when you consent. If we introduce a cookie-consent banner, it will store a single strictly necessary cookie to remember your preference, which does not require consent.

Analytics cookies (consent required)

Subject to your consent, we use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our Site so we can improve it. These cookies collect information in an aggregated, pseudonymised form.

Other third-party requests

  • Google Fonts — we load fonts from Google's servers to render the Site. This involves a request to Google but does not set cookies on your device.
  • Web3Forms — used to deliver contact-form submissions. It processes your submission when you send the form but does not set cookies on your device.
Note: Because we use analytics, UK law requires that non-essential cookies are only set after you opt in. Ensure a compliant cookie-consent banner is in place before Google Analytics is activated, so that analytics cookies load only after consent is given.

3. Managing your cookies

You can control cookies in several ways:

  • Consent controls: where a cookie banner is shown, you can accept or reject non-essential cookies and change your choice at any time.
  • Browser settings: most browsers let you block or delete cookies. See aboutcookies.org for guidance. Blocking some cookies may affect how the Site works.
  • Google Analytics opt-out: you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

4. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes to the cookies we use. The "Last updated" date shows the latest version.

5. Contact us

For questions about our use of cookies, contact us at privacy@agilists.co.uk.
